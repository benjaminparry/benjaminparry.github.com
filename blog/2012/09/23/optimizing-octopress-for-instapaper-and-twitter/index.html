Optimizing Octopress for Instapaper and Twitter

Sep 23rd, 2012

This is part of a talk I will be giving at BarCampBrighton 7 My attempt at the perfect read-later workflow

For a while I’ve been obsessed with nailing a perfect read-later workflow for online publishing. I use Instapaper a lot and do most of my reading, distraction-free, on my 3rd gen. Kindle. I joined IFTTT while in Beta and been using it as the missing link in this workflow. Lastly, my social media of choice is Twitter…for now anyway.

The aim of this workflow is to automate the read-later process as much as possible and document relevant posts for future reference.

Like most services now, Instapaper has a “Like” feature. If I read something that strikes a chord in me, I “Like” it. This is great from an archivist perspective but what else can I do with it? I don’t always have time to compose a Tweet to the author, to be honest I’d rather just open the next article. Besides, the reason I use a Kindle is because I don’t want to be distracted.

☆ Instapaper ☆ > Twitter

So I created this IFTTT recipe to post a Tweet once an Instapaper is “Liked”. I won’t go into how and why I chose to setup the recipe the way I did, I’ll leave that for another blog post coming soon. All I wanted to do is make sure I did something with that data, for me and the author.

I’m pretty sure Instapaper has a Tweet facility baked-in but I prefer to use IFTTT recipes to get a little more customization into that 140-character limit.

My ideal automated Tweet would have:

  1. Custom text
  2. Article title
  3. Article link
  4. Author name
  5. Twitter @ handle
  6. Content related hashtags per post

Using the Instapaper baked-in facility lets you do 2 and 3 easily enough, next up would be visiting the blog url and using a Tweet button and customizing the text, or you could just open up your nearest device and create exactly want you want.

I thought I might be able to at least get the author name from the Instapaper “Liked” RSS feed but apparently not, quite an odd omission for an online/offline read-later service. I had a crack at the Instapaper text parsing, but I must admit I had no idea what I was doing, please Tweet me if you can help me out at all.

So IFTTT can handle 1, 2 and 3 but it seems 4, 5 and 6 have to be hacked together somehow.

A blogging framework for hackers.

Enter Octopress. It’s been a perfect excuse to get started with Github and chance for me to explore the author’s place in this workflow.

The moment I finally got this together and tested I got the email from IFTTT informing of the closure of some of the Twitter related recipe services. Luckily this recipe is safe as posting content to Twitter is allowed but posting Twitter’s content to another service is not. A lucky escape, for now, although it does break some of my custom workflow that I will need to re-think.
